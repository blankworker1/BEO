# BEO Joint Programme Funding Request


---

## 1. Funding Overview

This document outlines the high-level funding requirements for the Bitcoin Energy Observatory (BEO) Joint Research Programme, comprising two complementary projects:

Project A: BEO Laboratory-Scale Research Project (Years 1–4)

Project B: BEO Agriculture Research Project (Years 2–5)


The funding request is structured to support the design, deployment, operation, and analysis of standardized BEO research installations, as well as open data publication and dissemination. Budgets are presented at a programme level, providing sufficient clarity for evaluation.


---

## 2. Joint Programme Funding Summary

### Total Programme Duration: 5 years

### Total Funding Requested (indicative): €3.2 – €3.8 million

This range reflects flexibility in system sizing, site logistics, and replication scope, while remaining aligned with comparable EU research infrastructure and applied research programmes.

Indicative Allocation

 Project A (Laboratory-Scale): €1.4 – €1.6 million

Project B (Agriculture): €1.8 – €2.2 million

---

### 5. Cost Efficiency and Synergies

The joint programme design delivers cost efficiency through:

Shared instrumentation standards and data pipelines

Reuse of validated hardware designs

Overlapping personnel expertise

Parallel data collection reducing total programme duration


These synergies reduce duplication while increasing scientific robustness.


---

### 6. Funding Rationale

The requested funding level reflects the ambition to create durable research infrastructure, not short-term experiments. The BEO programme prioritizes:

Measurement quality over scale

Longitudinal data over snapshots

Open science over proprietary outcomes


Investment at this level enables results that are policy-relevant, scientifically credible, and reusable beyond the initial funding period.


---

7. Conclusion

The BEO Joint Research Programme represents a focused, efficient investment in resolving foundational questions at the intersection of energy systems, land use, and digital infrastructure. The proposed funding supports both the creation of a novel scientific instrument and its application in real-world contexts, ensuring durable impact and high value for public research investment.
